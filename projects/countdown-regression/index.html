Countdown Regression: Sharp and Calibrated Survival Predictions.

Anand Avati, Tony Duan, Ken Jung, Nigam Shah, Andrew Ng

We build a program using Deep Learning to automatically identify hospitalized patients having palliative needs

Personalized probabilistic forecasts of time to event (such as mortality) can be crucial in decision making, especially in the clinical setting. Inspired by ideas from the meteorology literature, we approach this problem through the paradigm of maximizing sharpness of prediction distributions, subject to calibration. In regression problems, it has been shown that optimizing the continuous ranked probability score (CRPS) instead of maximum likelihood leads to sharper prediction distributions while maintaining calibration. We introduce the Survival-CRPS, a generalization of the CRPS to the time to event setting, and present right-censored and interval-censored variants.
